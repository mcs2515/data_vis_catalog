Dot Map

Mark:

Point

Channel:

Position

Dot Maps display geospatial data on a map. Each point on the map is positioned by its longitude and latitude. Each point, also, is given a description or label thus becoming a ‘point of interest’. Point maps are not great for showing exact values. However, they can reveal spatial patterns on the map.


There are two types Point Maps:

One-to-one: 1 point = a single count/object in the area.

One-to-Many: 1 point = several counts/objects in the area.
